Russia is more diverse than I thought

I always thought that Russia was just a bunch of East Slavic people with a whole lot of space, playing Counter Strike and sipping on Vodka. Obviously, most of Russia is empty and cold Siberia, but there are actually a heck lot of “countries” in Russia as well that took me by surprise. Just take a look at a map of all de jure republics within Russia

By Kwamikagami - Own work, CC BY-SA 4.0, Link

I think this is really interesting because each of these autonomous republics function as their own country. And it’s not like each Republic is the same, they all have their own unique quirks and characteristics.

Sakha Republic (Yakutia)

By File:Map of Russia - Sakha (Yakutia).svg: Stasyan117derivative work: Seryo93 - This file was derived from: Map of Russia - Sakha (Yakutia).svg: , CC BY-SA 4.0, Link

The Sakha Republic is huge, with a total area of 3,083,523 km^2. This means that if the Sakha Republic were an actual country, it would have been the 8th largest country in the world. It would have an area just shy of India’s area of 3,287,263 km^2!

The Sakha Republic is also infamous for having the coldest city in the world, Yakutsk. During winter the temperature in Yakutsk can reach as low as -64.4° C (-83.9° F) and consistently reaches below -20° C (-4° F)

By Ilya Varlamov - Own work, CC BY-SA 4.0, Link

Interestingly enough, the city of Yakutsk is 1.4 percent Chinese. The United States has approximately 5.5 million Chinese out of 342 million total people, meaning that both the United States and Yakutsk have around the same proportion of Chinese people. The majority of Yakutsk are of the Yakuts ethnicity (obviously) with the Russian ethnicity being the runner up.

Republic of Dagestan

Another interesting republic is Dagestan. Dagestan literally means “land of the mountains” which is a fitting name considering that a majority of the land is made up of mountains. By Sobolev Alexander - Own work, CC BY-SA 4.0, Link By Stianbh - based on Image:Dagestani flag.png., Public Domain, Link By Danloud - This vector image includes elements that have been taken or adapted from this file:, CC BY-SA 3.0, Link

Dagestan is located next to the Caspian Sea, also bordering Azerbaijan and Georgia. For some reason, Dagestan tends to produce some of the world’s greatest MMA fighters. Dagestan has produced Khabib Nurmagomedev, UFC lightweight champion, as well as Islam Makhachev, another former lightweight champion.
